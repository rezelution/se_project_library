Readers picks

That our readers love

  • The Count of Monte Cristo

    The Count of Monte Cristo" follows Edmond Dantès, a wrongfully imprisoned sailor who escapes from Château d'If, finds a hidden treasure, and seeks revenge on those who betrayed him. As he enacts his plans, he grapples with the moral implications of vengeance and ultimately discovers the importance of forgiveness and human connection.

    Alexander Dumas

  • The Lord of the Rings

    The Lord of the Rings" follows Frodo Baggins, a hobbit tasked with destroying the One Ring, a powerful artifact sought by the dark lord Sauron. Accompanied by a diverse fellowship, Frodo embarks on a perilous journey across Middle-earth, facing numerous challenges and temptations. Ultimately, the story explores themes of friendship, sacrifice, and the struggle between good and evil.

    John Ronald Reuel Tolkien

  • The World of Greek Mythology

    This book offers a comprehensive overview of Greek mythology, exploring its key figures, narratives, and themes. The book examines the pantheon of gods and goddesses, legendary heroes, and mythic creatures, illustrating how these stories reflect the values and beliefs of ancient Greek culture. Buxton highlights the enduring influence of these myths on art, literature, and modern thought.

    Richard Buxton

  • One Hundred Years of Solitude

    The novel follows the Buendía family in the mythical town of Macondo across several generations. It explores themes of solitude, love, and the cyclical nature of history through the family's triumphs and tragedies. Magic realism intertwines with their lives, reflecting broader social and political issues while highlighting the complex connections between personal and collective destinies.

    Gabriel García Márquez

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and the Triple Peaks Library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 monthly visitors. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and using our ample online resources.
